About me

Assimilating into new environments is second nature to me, having grown up immersed in the multinational artistic cultures of London and traveled across various countries such as India. My working background in the events and film industry taught me to be approachable, flexible and patient with any situation I am faced with. I spend a lot of my time focusing on self-improvement, be it exercise such as swimming, cycling or yoga, or enriching the mind with galleries and art exhibitions.

I hunger for the opportunity to demonstrate my quickness to learn as well as my love for building something out of nothing. The possibility of allowing my ideas to come to life in an environment where I am given the freedom to do so, and the knowledge and supervision to guide me is something that exhilarates me.
Notwithstanding one's circumstances or previous career paths, I believe everybody must be given a chance to fulfill their potential, as long as it is met with enthusiasm, positivity and a willingness to listen and take constructive criticism.

Why me

Why you should accept me

14 years ago, I became smitten with the visibly magical aspect of the technological processes behind design; this became an invaluable outlet through which I felt I could best express my creativity. Without the teaching and guidance of the cohort, I strongly feel I would be unable to develop my coding skills and perfect them in order to have the capacity to solve more advanced technical challenges.

Technology

With my finger on the pulse of new technology, I willingly share my knowledge and resources with those who need it. I know my drive to develop as a professional coder won't ever leave me, and I wish to learn and deepen my understanding of new programming languages, AI and machine learning. .

Communication

I always strive to communicate as clearly as possible. If I need assistance or to communicate my ideas, I am confident I am able to share my thoughts in the most constructive way possible. In this way it is possible to better foster group harmony. I am ready to challenge myself and make the push that will undoubtedly open doors to opportunities, connections and the changes I want to see in this exciting field.

Teamwork

The prospect of building a portfolio of projects with others in a bootcamp is something I already feel drawn to. Although problem-solving (such as working closely with others who may or not have the same ideas as me) is met with reluctance by many people, for me this is hugely enjoyable. And witnessing the fruits of my labour at the end is what makes any difficulty or frustration in a project worthwhile for me.

Challenges

The ability to not only code, but to code well has for many years been considered to be a key part of the future. However, this area is not without issues. Many people overlook how dominated the industry is by Caucasian males, and the wider environmental impact of increased programming and Internet use on resources. We need to address these challenges and brainstorm methodologies so that we can build a more diverse and cleaner future for this specific field. I would be honoured to make a contribution towards these essential shifts.
